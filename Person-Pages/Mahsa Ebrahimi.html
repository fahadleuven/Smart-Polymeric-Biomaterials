Mahsa Ebrahimi

Mahsa was born and raised in Tehran, Iran. She obtained her master’s degrees in Materials Science and Engineering from the University of Tehran. She completed her PhD in Polymer Chemistry through a joint program between Hasselt University (Belgium) and Maastricht University (the Netherlands). At Hasselt University, she was part of the Advanced Functional Polymers group under the supervision of Prof. Louis Pitet, and at Maastricht University she worked within the MERLN Institute under the supervision of Prof. Matt Baker and Prof. Lorenzo Moroni.

Her doctoral research focused on enhancing the mechanical performance of injectable and 3D-printable hydrogels for tissue engineering. She specifically investigated strategies to improve hydrogel network uniformity/mechanics using various chemistries, with an emphasis on load-bearing applications. Her work encompassed polymer synthesis, polymer characterization, and advanced processing techniques, including light-based 3D printing such as volumetric printing.

Following her PhD, Mahsa continued as a postdoctoral researcher in Prof. Pitet’s group, working on the depolymerization, chemical recycling, and upcycling of PET-based waste streams. In October 2025, she joined Prof. Arn Mignon’s group at KU Leuven for her second postdoctoral position, focusing on the development of hydrophilic polymer systems for processing via melt electrowriting for plant-sensor applications.

Her research interests include polymer synthesis, characterization, and processing.
