NO-Releasing Hydrogel Wound Dressing

Diabetic foot ulcers are one major complication (20-30%) of the disease. They are associated to increased risk of lower limb amputation (10%) derived from infection. They also increase mortality by 50-70% within 5 years. Current treatment requires wound dressings that not only protect by being a physical barrier, but that also maintain a moist environment while removing excessive exudate. Latest research focuses in developing composite dressings made of collagen, nanomaterials, chitosan, etc. with a bioactive capacity to actively aid in the wound healing process (Jiang et al., 2023).

Nitric oxide (NO) is an endogenous gas molecule produced in the body upon skin injury to regulate haemostasis, inflammation, and antimicrobial action. Diabetic patients present decreased NO levels leading to impaired healing and ulcers. Studies have proven that its topical application by NO-carriers stimulates cell proliferation, collagen production and angiogenesis, as well as reduced risk of infection (Sivaraj et al., 2023).

The focus of this project is to design and test a hydrogel wound dressing, chemically modified to release NO according to the wound’s deficiency by enzymatic action. The dressing is processed by electrospinning (Figure 1) for the breathability and emulation of the natural extracellular matrix. This material is tested on its biological properties, as well as on its mechanical performance (Figure 2).
