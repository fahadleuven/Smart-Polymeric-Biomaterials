Thibault Vervenne

Thibault Vervenne is a PhD Candidate and Research Associate in Soft Tissue Biomechanics, under the supervision of Prof. Nele Famaey. He is part of the Department of Mechanical Engineering at KU Leuven, in the Biomechanics division (BMe). Thibault is co-supervised by Prof. Arn Mignon as part of the Smart Polymeric Biomaterials research group at KU Leuven Campus Group T, and by Prof. Filip Rega at the Department of Cardiovascular Sciences (University Hospitals Leuven). Thibault is a research expert at FIBEr, the KU Leuven core facility for biomechanical experimentation. He is also an active visiting researcher at Stanford University, in the Living Matter Lab of Prof. Ellen Kuhl, as part of Stanford Bio-X and the Stanford Department of Mechanical Engineering.

Thibault’s research passion lies at the intersection of engineering, medicine, and materials science. He works within this scientific triangle by combining three pillars: (I) computer models and simulations in biomechanical engineering, (II) clinical applications in cardiac surgery, and (III) polymeric medical textiles for cardiovascular interventions. In his PhD, Thibault focusses on the development of a mechanocompatible external support for the Ross procedure. To achieve this, he uses computational modeling to simulate the growth and remodeling of arterial tissue, to predict long-term outcomes after surgery. As input for these models, multiaxial mechanical testing is used to characterize the material behavior of cardiovascular soft tissues. Thibault designed a mechanocompatible polymeric construct using electrospinning technology, to serve as biodegradable external support around the artery. This textile has been successfully evaluated in a pre-clinical setting, demonstrating promising performance for future studies.

Thibault is funded by the Research Foundation Flanders (FWO) through a strategic basic PhD fellowship. He is the 2024 audience & jury 1st prize winner at the Falling Walls Lab pitch competition in Leuven. Finally, Thibault is an active triathlete and certified Emergency Medical Technician, providing prehospital urgent care in the Leuven area.
